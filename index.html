Nutrition Myths vs Facts

Myth: "Eating fruit on an empty stomach prevents blood sugar spikes."

Fact: While fruit contains natural sugars, eating it alone can still cause glucose spikes. Pairing fruit with protein, fiber, or healthy fats (like nuts or yogurt) helps slow down sugar absorption and stabilize blood sugar levels.

Myth: "All calories affect the body the same way."

Fact: Calories from different foods impact glucose levels differently. 100 calories from white bread will cause a much higher glucose spike than 100 calories from nuts, which have fiber and fat that slow glucose absorption. Managing glucose is more about food order and composition than just calorie counting.

Myth: "Skipping breakfast helps with weight loss and glucose control."

Fact: Skipping breakfast can lead to higher glucose spikes later in the day, increasing cravings and energy crashes. A balanced breakfast with protein, fiber, and healthy fats helps keep glucose stable and prevents overeating later.
